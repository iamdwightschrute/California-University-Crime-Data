2015 California Campus Crime Report

This report utilizes data encompassing crime statistics from various campuses across California, providing insights into patterns and correlations related to crime on university campuses. The data used includes violent crimes, property crimes, student enrollment numbers, and specific types of crimes such as burglary and larceny-theft. The analysis performed utilizes various graphical representations to depict the distribution and nature of campus crimes, helping to understand the scale and types of criminal activities affecting these institutions.

Violent Crimes by Campus

This bar chart displays the total number of violent crimes reported by each University of California campus.

Findings:

The graph provides a comparative view of violent crime occurrences across campuses, revealing which locations experience higher rates. This helps in identifying campuses with significant safety concerns and potentially guides resource allocation for crime prevention and safety measures.

Pie Chart of Crime Types per Campus(University of California and California State University)

Pie chart showing the proportion of different types of crimes at San Jose campus.

Select a campus to view the distribution of different types of crimes.

Findings:

This visualization breaks down crime types at San Jose campus, highlighting a predominant prevalence of larceny-theft, which constitutes 84.6% of the crimes. Such data can prioritize focus areas for campus security measures and student safety initiatives.

Crime Composition by Campus

Bar chart comparing violent and property crime counts across various campuses.

Findings:

This chart details the composition of crimes at different campuses, distinguishing between property and violent crimes. It allows for a nuanced understanding of the campus environments, showing how crime types vary significantly across different locations, which is crucial for targeted prevention strategies.

Correlation Matrix of Campus Crime Data

Visual representation of the correlation coefficients between different crime metrics and student enrollment.

Findings:

The correlation matrix reveals a strong positive relationship between student enrollment and both property and violent crimes across universities. Specifically, property crime exhibits a very high correlation of 0.98, and violent crime shows a similarly strong correlation of 0.96 with student enrollment. This suggests that higher student populations may be associated with increased incidences of these crimes at universities.

Histogram of Larceny-Theft Crimes

Histogram showing the distribution of larceny-theft crime counts across various campuses.

Findings:

This histogram delineates the frequency distribution of larceny-theft incidents across campuses, which helps in identifying crime hotspots and can be used to deploy specific anti-theft measures and awareness programs at campuses with higher occurrences.

Data sourced from the Uniform Crime Reporting (UCR) Program.
